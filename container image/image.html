Flowers, also called blooms or blossoms, are the reproductive parts of flowering plants (Angiosperms) that produce seeds and fruit. They typically consist of four main parts: protective sepals, colorful petals, male stamens (which produce pollen), and female pistils (which receive pollen to fertilize ovules). Flowers come in a vast variety of shapes, sizes, and colors, and their primary role is to attract pollinators like insects and birds to facilitate reproduction.
